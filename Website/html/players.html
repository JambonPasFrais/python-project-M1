Cleveland Cavaliers

This pie chart allows us to display the repartition of rebounds (offensive and defensive), free throws (attempted and made), field goals (attempted and made) and personal fouls for the latest season.
However, we do have to notice that the plots must be interpreted with the position of the player. For example, centre players may not have the same offensive rebounds as shooting guard players.
The plus/minus value is a score-base metric given to a certain player, depending on the team’s performance. It measures the difference between points scored and points lost when the player is on the court.
Higher the score is, better the player is. We decided to calculate the mean of all the coefficients of a player since he started to play in NBA. That shows if the player can bring an advantage to his team or not.
At the end of each season, a player will have a rank set by the number of points he has.
With this graph you could see if the player improved himself, and if his age has an impact on his performances.
Individual win rate is interesting to study. It allows us to know if the player has chances to win a game when he enters on the court, and if the player can have strong impact on the game or not, thing that + / - can’t tell us.
Like the other charts in the player section, those bars tell us how good the player is when he is playing.
This spider chart shows us if the player is better when he is playing at home or not.
For a team strategy, we can use this information to know when send the player to the court in order to maximize the chances to win the game and to maximize player’s performances.
The graph is useful as we can see easily where the weaknesses of the player are depending on the place he is playing in.
